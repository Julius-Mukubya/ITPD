**Effective Date:** February 2, 2026

## 1. Introduction

WellPath Mental Health Platform ("WellPath," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy and maintaining the confidentiality of your personal and health information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our mental health platform and services.

**Your privacy is fundamental to our mission of providing safe, effective mental health support.**

## 7. Data Retention and Deletion

### 7.1 Retention Periods

**Active Accounts:**
- Account information: Retained while account is active
- Health records: 7 years after last treatment (or longer if required)
- Communication logs: 3 years for quality assurance
- Usage analytics: 2 years for service improvement
- Crisis intervention records: 10 years for safety purposes

**Inactive Accounts:**
- Accounts inactive for 3 years may be archived
- Health information retained per legal requirements
- Non-health data may be deleted after 1 year of inactivity
- Users notified before any deletion occurs

### 7.2 Deletion Procedures
When you request deletion or close your account:
- Personal identifiers removed within 30 days
- Health records retained per legal requirements
- Anonymized data may be retained for research
- Backups purged according to retention schedule
- Third parties notified to delete shared data

### 7.3 Legal Holds
We may retain information longer when:
- Required by law or regulation
- Subject to litigation or investigation
- Necessary for safety or security purposes
- Part of ongoing treatment relationships
- Needed for regulatory compliance

## 14. Definitions

**Personal Information:** Any information that identifies or can be used to identify an individual.

**Health Information:** Information about physical or mental health, healthcare services, or payment for healthcare.

**De-identified Data:** Information that has been processed to remove personal identifiers.

**Pseudonymized Data:** Information processed so that it cannot be attributed to a specific person without additional information.

**Data Controller:** The entity that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data.

**Data Processor:** The entity that processes personal data on behalf of the data controller.
